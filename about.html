The Unbeatable Game

How many times did you have to play to realize that this game was unbeatable?
Like the disappointment and frustration you may have felt playing, the game
alludes to how sexual assault survivors may feel when they are let down by our
justice system. Problems within the system only make the issue regarding sexual assault
a more pervasive issue.

Many survivors are often times very hesitant in reporting cases of sexual assault.
Reportedly over 80% of survivors do not report any incidents to authorities due to
fear of judgment. It is especially hard to win a case when you do not have additional
outside support. In the off chance that a case is taken to trial, which is 8%, further
problems arise. The victims have to provide a testimony of their experience, which at times
may be the only plausible piece of evidence they have. This process of trying to retrigger
memories of the incident is very traumatizing and difficult to procure. As a result, it is
inevitable for their to be discrepancies within their story from different accounts, allowing
their credibility to be undermined. If a case closes and the offender charged, alcohol makes this
entire process difficult as it can be used a mitigating to receive a lighter sentence.

Survivor's logo is a teal ribbon which is used to raise awareness for rape. I was inspired to
create this website because of a recent issue in South Korea. Recently the release of a child rapist
after being in jail for 12 years has been making headlines in the country. On December 11, 2008,
Cho Doo Soon beat and raped an 8-year-old girl and was left permanently disabeled with
permanent damage to her lower abdomen. She also suffered from depression and other mental
health issues following the incident. People flew to the streets to protest his release, criticizing his
lenient sentence. Cho was supposed serve a life sentence but had it reduced after the victim
testified that the offender may have been under the influence.

Reform begins with breaking the stigmas surrounding sexual assault which extends to all
genders, races, and ages.
